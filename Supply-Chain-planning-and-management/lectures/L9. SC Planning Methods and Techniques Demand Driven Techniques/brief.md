# L1. Introduction and overview

In this week’s lecture, we introduce the **Supply Chain Planning and Management** module. We will discuss the structure and general content of the module, module aims, learning objectives, and assessment, as well as introduce the teaching team. We will also begin our first topic, which introduces the basic components of supply chain management and the importance of supply chain planning.

Please come to the lecture having engaged with the learning activities below.

---

## Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

- Identify the key components and flows in a supply chain
- Define the basic components of supply chain management
- Understand the importance of planning in supply chains
- Describe the differences between **Make-to-Stock (MTS)** and **Make-to-Order (MTO)** systems and understand when they are likely to be used

### For the Self-Study Session, You Should Be Able To:

- Describe the **Customer Order Decoupling Point (CODP)** and explain its importance in operations and supply chain systems
- Understand some of the factors affecting today’s supply chains and the impact of digital technologies on the supply chain

---

## Learning Activities

- Read the introductory chapter of one of the recommended textbooks presented in the reading list
- Think of examples of supply chains and note down why planning is important for these supply chains
- Review all teaching materials provided for Session 1
